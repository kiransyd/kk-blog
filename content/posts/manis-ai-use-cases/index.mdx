Stop chatting with AI. Start delegating work to it.

Meta just spent billions on a company whose flagship product—Mani's AI—does something most “chat assistants” still can’t: it opens tabs, clicks buttons, fills forms, sends emails, posts in Slack, even books calendars. It behaves like a digital employee. After watching Mani's blitz to $100M ARR in eight months (the fastest startup ramp I’ve seen), I made it part of my ops stack. Here’s how I’m using it to replace entire roles right now.

## 1. Ship a $10k website before your coffee cools

Remember spending three weeks and $5–10k to get a landing page live? Now it’s a 20-minute pass:

- Voice-prompt the “Build Website” agent with pages, colors, and a reference URL.
- Stay inside Mani's while it scaffolds layout, copy, and assets; iterate by talking to it.
- Connect the deployment link to GitHub/custom domain.

**Old way:** 4 weeks + $10k.
**Mani's way:** 20 minutes + $39/month.

## 2. Launch a working app without hiring devs

Client portals, intake tools, internal dashboards—I used to burn six figures before validating. New flow:

1. Map the workflow (users, inputs, outputs, rules).
2. Load the “Develop Apps” agent with the script + napkin mockups.
3. Grab the live deploy link, share it with the team, keep iterating.

I even build disposable apps for week-long sprints: ship → use → archive. That would have cost me $10–50k in the agency era.

## 3. Infinite content machine (no more blank docs)

Instead of spending Monday staring at Notion, I run the “Wide Research” agent:

> “Target: busy professionals. Study the top 20 fitness creators, find outlier posts, and build me a 30-day calendar for Instagram + LinkedIn with hooks, captions, CTAs, and best posting times.”

The agent scrapes, scores, and outputs a calendar pre-validated by what’s already winning. I spend 15 minutes approving hooks instead of 15 hours ideating.

## 4. Competitor intel on autopilot

Andy Grove’s mantra—“only the paranoid survive”—finally has leverage:

- Feed Mani's your top five rivals.
- Ask for pricing, positioning, reviews, social strategy, ad library pulls, and site architecture.
- Get a sheet + mini deck with the five biggest differentiation plays.

Research time: 20 hours → 30 minutes. I no longer walk across the highway blindfolded.

## 5. Hire while you sleep

Traditional hiring = 25 hours of résumé triage + recruiter fees. Now I:

1. Prompt Mani's to build a role intake form.
2. Fill it once.
3. Let it source, rank, and draft outreach to top-fit candidates.

Result: 45 minutes of my time, zero recruiter tax, consistent pipeline.

## 6. Leads for days (plus first-touch emails)

Lead gen used to be the most soul-sucking spreadsheet grind. Now I say:

> “Find 200 U.S. ecom brands doing $1–10M ARR, pull the CEO or marketing director, email, LinkedIn, and company URL. Save as CSV.”

Then:

- Connect Gmail, ask for personalized three-sentence outreach referencing LinkedIn or site.
- Approve a batch of 10, test, then scale.

Full 200-lead pass now takes ~90 minutes, not 60 man-hours or a $20k agency retainer.

## 7. Revive “dead” deals automatically

“Just checking in” kills more revenue than pricing. Mani's now audits my CRM:

- Filter opps with zero activity in 30+ days.
- Analyze the last touch, guess why it stalled, find a relevant article/case study.
- Draft a friendly founder-style nudge and hit send.
- Schedule a recurring “revive dead leads” block + DM me the summary.

I recover 25–50k in “lost” revenue every quarter because follow-up actually happens.

## Bonus: Mani's in Telegram = a chief of staff on your phone

Hook Mani's into Telegram and it becomes proactive: every morning it reviews my goals, inbox, and calendar, then pitches what it can knock out before I finish espresso. I reply with voice memos, it opens tabs, books meetings, writes docs, and reports back. It’s the first time AI genuinely feels like a teammate—not a chat box.

**TL;DR**

- 20 minutes to ship what used to cost $10–50k.
- Content, competitor intel, hiring, lead gen, and pipeline revival all run via one promptable agent.
- Mani's doesn’t advise—it executes. Treat it like the digital employee it is.

Pick one of the seven workflows above and delegate it to Mani's today. Once you feel what it’s like to have a $39/month teammate, you won’t go back to waiting on freelancers.
